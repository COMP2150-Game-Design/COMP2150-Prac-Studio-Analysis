# Week 12 - Studio Analysis
In today's class, you'll be doing some light research into the practicalities of design and development for different types of games.

This is a little different to the other pracs, but we hope it'll be a fun and informative way to end this part of the unit (lectures and SGTAs will still run in Week 13).

* Search engines

## Task 02 - Classic Game Design (20 min)
In pairs, select from the below list of "classic" games:
* Mortal Kombat
* Pac-Man
* Tetris
* DOOM
* A Mind Forever Voyaging
* Yar's Revenge

For your selected game, set about researching the design and development of the game. Make some notes that answer these questions:
* What was the vision behind this game? How did it come to be?
* Who was leading the project? Was there a single "mastermind", or more a small team of personalities?
* How many people were involved in making the game? What were their roles?
* What challenges did the team face? How did they overcome them?

You want to try and find articles, post-mortems, and other artefacts from the game. You might have played it before, or be able to find a (legal) way to play it now.

Your instructor will lead you all in presenting your findings.

## Task 03 - Contemporary Game Design (20 min)
In pairs, briefly discuss what games released in the last ~10 years interest you. Pick one (maybe only one of you has played it, that's okay!). Then, conduct the same kind of research, trying to answer:
* What seem to be the main themes behind this game? Does it fit any particular wave of genre or story telling trends?
* Was the game created by an independent studio, or someone tied to larger publisher/company?
* Does the project have a distinctive lead? What does their job appear to be in terms of making the game happen?
* How many people were involved in making the game? What were their roles?
* What challenges did the team face? How did they overcome them?

Your instructor will lead you all in presenting your findings.

## Task 04 - Indie, Outsider, and "Art" Games (20 min)
In pairs, have a look on [itch.io](itch.io). This is a website where anyone can publish their games, with the content ranging from tiny art pieces to full-blown commercial games. Some games you might know (Night In The Woods, Celeste) have lived here at one point or another.

Explore some of the tags to find a game that interests you. Try to stick to the "Top Rated" games for your category, as you'll otherwise be going to a lot of pages that make this task difficult.

Take a look at the credits, and explore the creator pages of the game makers (note: the page that has published the game is probably the lead or a small studio, and if there is no other indication of creators, this might be all there is). If you come across a creator that doesn't have many games, move on to another one. Then, try to answer:

* Is there a consistent theme among these games? What kind of game does the creator seem to like making?
* What skills does the creator appear to have? If you look at their other games, can you see any hints of collaboration with other creators?
* Looking at the creator's page and their game descriptions, is there any sense of the values or design philosophies the creator has? What do you think matters to them as a creator?

Your instructor will lead you all in presenting your findings.

## Task 05 - Your Dream Game
With all of this in mind, chat to your partner about the kind of game you think you'd like to design and develop. Did anything you see today inspire you one way or the other? Then consider the practicalities:
* How big a team do you think you would need?
* What could you handle yourself, and what would you need some help with?
* What do you think would be your biggest challenges to get this game made?

We will finish class with you pitching this game and its practicalities to the rest of the class. If there is time, the class will vote on the game they consider the most feasible.
